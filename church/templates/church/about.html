About Us

A community committed to making Jesus Christ known

We are a community committed to the Lord Jesus Christ. Our aim is to make Jesus Christ known to everyone and to help you to grow in love for him and belief in him. We warmly welcome you to join our church services and small groups. We have weekly meetings in English and Cantonese.

What We Believe

The foundational truths that guide our faith and practice

1. The Bible

The Bible is inspired by God, inerrant in the original documents and is the final authority in all matters of faith and practice.

2. The Trinity

There is one God, revealed in 3 persons who are co-existent, co-equal and co-eternal – the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit.

3. Jesus Christ

The Lord Jesus Christ is truly God and truly man. He was conceived by the Holy Spirit and was born of the virgin Mary. Although he was sinless, he died on the cross for our sins, was buried and rose bodily from the dead, and ascended to His Father's right hand. We believe in the return of Jesus Christ, ushering in the ultimate rule of God and His judgment on mankind.

4. Salvation

All mankind is sinful and of corrupt nature since the Fall, rendering them subject to God's wrath and condemnation. Christ alone offers redemption through His death. The redemption becomes effective through repentance of sin and acceptance of Christ as Lord and Saviour by faith. Its reality is manifested by the subsequent growth of a believer.

5. The Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit lives in every believer and effects his/her regeneration.

6. The Church

The Universal Church consists of all true believers who have experienced God's redemption through Jesus Christ.

7. Christ is the Head

Christ is the head of the Universal Church and this church.

Our Story

The Canberra Austral-Asian Christian Church was formed in 1984. Right from the beginning, we have been a people characterised by unity amidst diversity. We are diverse in age, cultural background, mother tongue, life experiences, education and social-economic background. But we are all united in and by Jesus Christ. He has been and will continue to be the head of our church, our senior pastor, Saviour, Lord and guide.

Our Ministries

Serving God and growing together

Kids Church

At Kids Church, we seek to introduce children to God their Heavenly Father and loving Creator, to Jesus Christ their Saviour and King, and to the Holy Spirit their wise Helper. We also aim to positively model God's family and help families actively contribute to the spiritual growth of their children.

Kids join the main service for singing and a short kids' talk ('Kids Spot'), then break off into small groups: Sprouts (toddlers), Seedlings (preschool to Year 2) and Saplings (Years 3 to 6). Each week's 'big idea' (teaching point) is reinforced through time spent reading or hearing from the Bible, games, activities and songs.

Youth Ministry

Our high schoolers meet fortnightly during the English service, to read and learn from the Bible. Our youth Bible studies follow the sermons, using the COMA acronym as well as church's Bible study notes, to grow our understanding of Scripture and its unifying gospel message.

Music and AV Ministry

The music team's vision is to serve the church through leading our congregation in songs of praise to Him. As such, we as music team members value first and foremost our relationship with Jesus Christ as our Saviour, and our commitment to serving the gospel to edify the church and reach others who do not yet know Him. All the music-related things that we do then, flow from this.

Prayer Ministry

What a privilege to carry everything to God in prayer! At CAACC, we are a prayerful church family, with our brothers and sisters coming together regularly for our monthly prayer meetings to pray for each other, for the church and for the world – all for God's glory. We've had the privilege of praying alongside the global church for the International Day of Prayer for the Persecuted Church, and other prayer events including prayer walks and letters of prayer. We continue to pray for more prayer – in our individual Christian walks and as a church family – as a way of calling on and communicating with our God and loving Heavenly Father, that we might continue to also grow in holiness and draw closer in our relationships with Him and with each other.

Cell Groups

Cell Groups are where we really get to know people and invest in one another's lives. It's where we connect for Bible study, prayer, and friendship. Cell Groups are where we think the best transformation happens, and a vital part of how we care for one another - to live as a redeemed community who love and care for one another, and point each other to Jesus.

Our groups meet throughout the city, on different nights of the week. They're a great first step into church if you're looking for a way to connect with some people who are committed to each other, and committed to working out who Jesus is. You don't have to have worked this out to be part of a group.
